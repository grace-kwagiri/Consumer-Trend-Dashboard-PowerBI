# 📊 Consumer Trend Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)

## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes five years of sales data (2007–2011) across multiple markets, cities, product lines, and customer segments to uncover key business trends and performance drivers.

Using Power BI, I developed an interactive dashboard to explore revenue patterns, customer behavior, geographic performance, and product profitability.

The goal is to support data-driven decision-making in sales strategy, marketing, and business growth.

---

## 📌 Problem Statement

Businesses generate large volumes of sales data, but without structured analysis, it becomes difficult to identify growth opportunities, optimize performance, and manage risks.

This project aims to analyze historical sales data to:

- Identify revenue and profit trends over time  
- Understand seasonal demand patterns  
- Evaluate geographic and market performance  
- Detect customer concentration risks  
- Analyze product-level profitability  
- Provide actionable insights for strategic decision-making  

---

## 🗂️ Dataset Description

- Time Period: **2007 – 2011**
- Markets: **Germany (DE) and United States (US)**
- Coverage:
  - 23 cities  
  - Multiple product categories  
  - Customer accounts  
  - Sales organizations  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- **Power BI** (Dashboard development & visualization)
- **DAX** (Data modeling & measures)
- **Excel / Data preprocessing**
- Data Analysis & Business Intelligence

---

## 📈 Key Metrics

- **Total Revenue:** $285M  
- **Total Profit:** $149M  
- **Profit Margin:** 52%  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 💰 Financial Performance
- Strong overall profitability with a **52% profit margin**  
- High average discounting per customer, indicating pricing flexibility  
- Discounts applied broadly across orders, suggesting potential margin leakage  

---

### 📅 Seasonal Trends
- Clear and consistent seasonal pattern:
  - **Peak:** May–June ($10M–$14M/month)  
  - **Low:** January–February ($1M–$2M/month)  
- Seasonal demand is predictable and can be optimized through planning  

---

### 🌍 Geographic Performance
- **Germany dominates** with ~59% of total profit  
- Strong cities: Munich, Hamburg  
- US market underdeveloped despite strong cities like Boston and Palo Alto  
- Several cities show **untapped growth potential**  

---

### 🏢 Sales Organization Performance
- DN00 leads with $88M revenue  
- UE00 & DS00 are strong mid-tier performers  
- UW00 significantly underperforms, indicating inefficiencies  

---

### 👥 Customer Insights
- Revenue is highly concentrated among top customers  
- Top 5 customers contribute a significant portion of total revenue  
- Largest customer alone contributes over **11% of total revenue**  
- Indicates **high dependency risk**  

---

### 🚲 Product Performance
- Top revenue driver: **Professional Road Bike**  
- Highest margin product: **Men’s Off Road Bike (Hard Tail)**  
- Strong performers across touring bike categories  

---

### 📉 Year-on-Year Trends
- Revenue dip observed in 2009 (economic impact)  
- Recovery seen in 2010–2011  
- Growth has stabilized but not significantly accelerated  

---

## 📊 Dashboard Features

- Interactive filters (Year, Market, Product, Customer)  
- Revenue & profit trend analysis  
- Geographic performance visualization  
- Product-level performance breakdown  
- Customer contribution analysis  
- Seasonal trend tracking  

---

## 🚀 Business Impact

This analysis provides actionable insights to:

- Improve **seasonal sales planning and inventory management**  
- Reduce **customer concentration risk**  
- Optimize **discounting strategies** to protect margins  
- Identify **high-growth geographic markets**  
- Prioritize **high-margin products**  
- Enhance **sales organization performance**  

---

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations

- Launch targeted campaigns ahead of peak seasons  
- Expand investment in underdeveloped US markets  
- Implement key account management for top customers  
- Review and optimize discounting strategy  
- Focus on high-margin product lines  
- Improve demand forecasting using historical data
